Invitation to Join The Conversation

Purpose

The purpose of this effort is to ask you to be cyber architects and envision what you think this park (really any park, but, yes this park) would need to to prove its programs, justify itself, interact with the citizens, and help our mental health. This is a brainstorm conversation that will prove the point that we can not only plan for the physical infrastructure of the park but also the cyber infrastructure of the park. We are looking for ideas about gathering data.

Backstory

In the 1850s and 60s 70s there was a woman Dorothy Dix who won and earned a grant of land from the North Carolina legislature. I think it was two thousand acres two thousand acres set aside for mental health. It is now whittled down to three hundred acres, so we know this kind of whittling happens over time. Nowadays thereis no longer a mental hospital on campus. It was a necessary decision to move the hospital away from this spot in the middle of Raleigh. A lot of people are still hurt by the lack of a mental health support services. But people involved in the park now see the park as a way to at least do something. Maybe the park can house some of these non-profit agencies that support mental health. Or another big idea is to provide places for support group meet ups. Maybe you you need a support group, or maybe you're a caretaker like you care take of somebody with a mental illness like Alzheimer's: you just need a support group. Or spaces for civic engagement meetings.This. idea to use the buildings for support group space has not really floated to the surface yet, but it's out there. What's happening right now with the park is that a master plan has been conceived, but one that is not explicitly consciousness about technology.

A Few Ideas from Civic Camp 2019

  • infrared or thermal sensors to count pedestrians at access points can quantify the populatrity of a park feature (without endangering privacy)
  • it's key to install sensors now for before-and-after perspectives on new installations
  • activity detection also helps mitigate crime
  • sensors on park benches and picnic tables can also detect usage
  • stand-alone kiosks can ask with a smiley or neutral or frowny face about how the feature made a visitor feel; no personal smart phone required
  • leverage the data from wireless provides about usage of phones over the park
  • leverage conference apps for large annual festival for mental health advocacy
  • leverage Pokemon data
  • provide outdoor, solor-powered charging stations
  • use augmented reality to present the landscape as it appeared in times gone by
  • publish attribute data on exsiting structures
  • create an app with a self-guided tour
  • use cameras with intereting activity targets, like the sunflowers
  • prompt visitors to use already-available apps for reporting flora and fauna
  • use displays at picnic tables to prompt visits to the farmer's market
  • at the farmer's market, show available picnic areas
  • use typical co-working space management software to manage the room rentals for meetings in the co-organizating spaces
  • explore how the park and the hosted organizations may appropriately share
  • develop an app to help find a mental health resource on park premises
