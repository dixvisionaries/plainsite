Invitation to Join The Brainstorm

You are invited!

The purpose of this site is to ask you to be cyberscape architects and envision what you think Dorothea Dix Park (really any park, but, yes this park) would need to continue and evolve its worthwhile programming, spaces, features, and interaction with the communities that support it by using technology: data, apps, and the internet of things (sensors). What's not "just any park" about this park is that it is one of the few parks in the world named after a woman, Dorothea Dix, who was an advocate for mental health reform. Wellness is the park's theme. Below is the starter brainstorm list of cyberscape possibilities from Civic Camp 2019, but you are invited to join our brainstorm. Next, we will develop a blog and API to futher collect ideas and resources. Join us at an upcoming Triangle Code for America meetup.

A Few Ideas from Civic Camp 2019

  • infrared or thermal sensors to count pedestrians at access points can quantify the populatrity of a park feature (without endangering privacy)
  • it's key to install sensors now for before-and-after perspectives on new installations
  • activity detection also helps mitigate crime
  • sensors on park benches and picnic tables can also detect usage
  • stand-alone kiosks can ask with a smiley or neutral or frowny face about how the feature made a visitor feel; no personal smart phone required
  • leverage the data from wireless provides about usage of phones over the park
  • leverage conference apps for large annual festival for mental health advocacy
  • leverage Pokemon data
  • provide outdoor, solor-powered charging stations
  • use augmented reality to present the landscape as it appeared in times gone by
  • publish attribute data on exsiting structures
  • create an app with a self-guided tour
  • use cameras with intereting activity targets, like the sunflowers
  • prompt visitors to use already-available apps for reporting flora and fauna
  • use displays at picnic tables to prompt visits to the farmer's market
  • at the farmer's market, show available picnic areas
  • use typical co-working space management software to manage the room rentals for meetings in the co-organizating spaces
  • explore how the park and the hosted organizations may appropriately and cooperatively share data
  • develop an app to help find a mental health resource on park premises
